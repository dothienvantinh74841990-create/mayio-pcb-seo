What Are Aluminum PCBs?

Understanding metal core PCB technology for superior thermal performance

Aluminum PCBs (also known as Metal Core PCBs or MCPCBs) are specialized circuit boards that replace the standard FR-4 fiberglass base with an aluminum substrate. This aluminum base layer provides exceptional thermal conductivity, enabling efficient heat dissipation from high-power components.


Unlike traditional PCBs, aluminum PCBs feature a unique three-layer structure: a copper circuit layer, a dielectric layer with thermal properties, and the aluminum base. This design allows heat to transfer away from sensitive components 8–10× faster than standard FR-4 boards, preventing thermal damage and extending product lifespan.


At Mayio, we manufacture aluminum PCBs with thermal conductivity options from 1.0 to 3.0 W/mK, supporting copper weights up to 6 oz, and board thicknesses from 0.4mm to 3.0mm. Whether you need single-sided, double-sided, or multilayer aluminum PCBs, our advanced manufacturing facilities deliver consistent quality at competitive prices.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about aluminum PCB manufacturing

What is an aluminum PCB?

An aluminum PCB (also called metal core PCB or MCPCB) is a type of circuit board that uses an aluminum base layer instead of the standard FR-4 fiberglass. The aluminum base provides excellent thermal conductivity, making these boards ideal for applications that generate significant heat, such as LED lighting, power converters, and automotive electronics.

How much does aluminum PCB manufacturing cost?

Aluminum PCB pricing starts from $5 for prototype quantities (5 pcs, 100×100mm). Production pricing ranges from $0.50–$2.00 per board depending on layer count, copper weight, thermal conductivity requirements, and quantity. Volume discounts are available for orders over 100 pcs.

What are aluminum PCBs used for?

Aluminum PCBs are primarily used in LED lighting (street lights, automotive headlights, panel lights), power electronics (DC-DC converters, motor drives, power supplies), automotive electronics (ignition systems, engine controls), and industrial applications where heat dissipation is critical.

What is the thermal conductivity of your aluminum PCBs?

We offer aluminum PCBs with thermal conductivity options from 1.0 W/mK to 3.0 W/mK. Standard aluminum PCBs use 1.0–1.5 W/mK dielectric, while high-performance options use 2.0–3.0 W/mK for demanding thermal applications.
