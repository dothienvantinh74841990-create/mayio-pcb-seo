What Is an SMT Stencil?

The critical first step in reliable surface mount assembly

An SMT stencil (Surface Mount Technology stencil) is a thin sheet of stainless steel with precision laser-cut apertures that match the pad locations on your PCB. It serves as a template for applying solder paste to the board before component placement.


During the assembly process, the stencil is aligned over the PCB, and a squeegee draws solder paste across the surface. The paste passes through the apertures and deposits precisely onto each pad. This ensures uniform, consistent solder deposits — critical for reliable solder joints, especially with fine-pitch components like QFPs, BGAs, and 0201 passives.


Without a quality stencil, you risk insufficient paste (cold joints), excess paste (bridging/shorts), or misaligned deposits — all of which lead to assembly defects and costly rework. At Mayio, we manufacture laser-cut stencils with ±0.01mm accuracy for consistently perfect solder paste deposits.

Common Stencil Mistakes to Avoid

Learn from others' mistakes to get perfect paste deposits

❌

Wrong Thickness

Using a stencil that's too thick causes bridging; too thin leads to insufficient paste. Match thickness to your smallest component pitch.

❌

Ignoring Aperture Design

Simply copying pad sizes doesn't work. Fine-pitch pads need reduced apertures (80–90%), while large pads may need expansion.

❌

Poor Stencil Cleaning

Dirty stencils cause paste clogging and inconsistent deposits. Clean stencils with IPA or dedicated stencil cleaner every 5–10 prints.

❌

Wrong Squeegee Pressure

Too much pressure scrapes paste from pads; too little leaves excess. Adjust pressure based on stencil type and paste viscosity.

❌

Misalignment

Poor stencil-to-board alignment causes offset paste deposits. Use fiducials for precise alignment in production environments.

❌

Skipping Paste Inspection

Always verify paste deposits visually or with SPI (Solder Paste Inspection) before reflow. Catching defects early saves costly rework.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about SMT stencils

What is an SMT stencil?

An SMT stencil (Surface Mount Technology stencil) is a thin sheet of stainless steel with laser-cut apertures that match the pad locations on a PCB. It is used to precisely apply solder paste to the board before component placement, ensuring consistent and accurate solder deposits for reliable SMT assembly.

How much does a PCB stencil cost?

SMT stencils start from $5 for standard frameless stencils and $15 for framed stencils. Pricing depends on stencil size, thickness (typically 0.08mm–0.15mm), and frame type. We offer FREE stencils with PCB assembly orders.

What stencil thickness should I use?

The most common stencil thickness is 0.12mm (4 mil), suitable for most standard SMT components. For fine-pitch components (0.4mm pitch QFPs, BGAs), use 0.08–0.10mm stencils. For larger components and power parts, 0.15mm stencils provide adequate paste volume.

Frameless vs framed stencils — which should I choose?

Frameless stencils are ideal for prototyping and low-volume production — they're cheaper and easier to store. Framed stencils are better for production environments as they hold tension in a stencil printer frame, ensuring consistent paste application across many boards.
