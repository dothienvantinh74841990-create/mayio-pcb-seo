Rigid-Flex PCB Manufacturing: Design Guide, Pricing & How to Order

Published June 10, 2026 · By Mayio Engineering Team · 9 min read

Your phone has one. Your smartwatch has one. Your hearing aid has one. Rigid-flex PCBs are the technology that makes modern compact electronics possible — they combine rigid circuit boards with flexible interconnects in a single, integrated assembly.

What Is Rigid-Flex?

Rigid-flex PCBs are hybrid boards that bond rigid FR4 sections with flexible polyimide sections. The result is a single board that can fold into a 3D shape — eliminating connectors, cables, and the reliability problems they cause.

  • Rigid sections — Where components are mounted (same as a standard PCB)
  • Flex sections — Thin, bendable areas that connect rigid sections
  • Bend zones — Where the flex layer transitions between rigid sections

  • Smartphones & tablets — Connecting display, battery, camera modules

Design Rules for Rigid-Flex

1. Bend Radius

  • Static bend (one-time): Minimum radius = 6× flex thickness
  • Dynamic bend (repeated): Minimum radius = 12× flex thickness

2. Copper Routing in Bend Zones

  • Route traces perpendicular to the bend axis
  • Use curved corners, never 90° angles
  • Keep copper weight thin (0.5oz) in flex areas
  • Use hatched pours instead of solid copper

3. Stiffeners

  • FR4 stiffeners for component mounting areas
  • Polyimide stiffeners for ZIF connector tails
  • Place stiffeners on the opposite side of components

4. Transitions

  • The rigid-to-flex transition is the most critical area
  • Use generous fillets at the transition edge
  • No vias or pads within 1mm of the transition
  • Coverlay should extend into the rigid section by at least 2mm

5. Drill-to-Edge Distance

  • Keep drills at least 0.5mm from any flex edge
  • Prefer mechanical drills (0.2mm+) over laser drills in flex areas

How to Order Rigid-Flex PCBs

  1. Design your board in KiCad/Altium with rigid and flex layers clearly defined
  2. Create a stackup drawing showing layer counts, materials, and thickness for each section
  3. Export Gerbers including all rigid and flex layers
  4. Send files + stackup drawing to your manufacturer
  5. Get DFM review — rigid-flex requires more review than standard boards
  6. Approve, pay, wait — typically 10-21 days for fabrication
