Overview: The Three Options

If you're getting a PCB made in 2026, you have three main paths:

  • JLCPCB — The fastest, cheapest instant-quote platform. Great for simple boards.
  • PCBWay — More flexible than JLCPCB, better for complex boards and assembly.
  • Custom manufacturer / partner — A direct relationship with a factory, often through a partner like Mayio. Best for production runs and startups.

None of these is universally "the best." The right choice depends on your project, your budget, and how much hand-holding you need.

The difference shows up with complex boards (6+ layers, flex, HDI) and assembly. Brokers markup these services significantly. Direct factory partners pass through the actual cost.

Quality & Reliability

JLCPCB

  • Good quality for standard boards
  • AOI (Automated Optical Inspection) included
  • DFM review is automated — catches obvious issues, misses subtle ones
  • Consistent because it's a high-volume automated process

PCBWay

  • Good quality, slightly more attention to detail than JLCPCB
  • Manual DFM review available (but slow)
  • More flexible with special processes

Custom / Direct Factory

  • Quality depends on the factory — this is why choosing the right partner matters
  • Human DFM review catches what automated tools miss
  • AOI + visual inspection before shipping
  • Better handling of edge cases (impedance control, tight tolerances)
The quality floor is similar across all three. The difference is in the ceiling — how well they handle your specific, non-standard requirements. For a simple 2-layer board, it barely matters. For a 6-layer board with impedance control, it matters a lot.

When to Choose Each

Choose JLCPCB when:

  • You want the cheapest possible prototype
  • Your board is simple (2-4 layers, standard specs)
  • You don't need engineering support
  • You're making a repeat order of a known-good design

Choose PCBWay when:

  • JLCPCB can't handle your specs (special materials, unusual thickness)
  • You need more flexibility in assembly sourcing
  • You're okay with paying more for slightly better support

Choose Mayio (or a direct partner) when:

  • You're a startup moving from prototype to production
  • You need engineering consultation on your design
  • You want a human reviewing your files, not a script
  • You need component sourcing from multiple suppliers
  • You want someone to manage the whole process (PCB + assembly + testing)
  • You're tired of being a ticket number
Bottom line: JLCPCB is great for quick, cheap prototypes. But when your project gets serious — production runs, assembly, quality requirements — a direct partner saves you time, money, and headaches.
