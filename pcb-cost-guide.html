What Determines PCB Cost?

PCB pricing isn't arbitrary. It follows predictable patterns based on six main factors:

📐 Layer Count

More layers = more manufacturing steps. Going from 2 to 4 layers roughly doubles the cost.

📦 Quantity

Setup costs are spread across more boards. 50pcs cost much less per board than 5pcs.

📏 Board Size

Larger boards use more material and fit fewer per panel. Price scales with area.

🧪 Material

FR4 is cheapest. Rogers, polyimide, and metal-core cost significantly more.

✨ Surface Finish

HASL is cheapest. ENIG adds $5-15. Hard gold for edge connectors adds more.

⚡ Turnaround

Standard (5-7 days) is cheapest. 24-hour express adds 50-100% to the price.

Layer Count: The Biggest Price Factor

Layer count is the single biggest driver of PCB cost. Each additional layer requires another cycle of lamination, drilling, and plating.

Rule of thumb: Doubling the layer count roughly doubles the cost. If you're on a budget, design for 2 layers whenever possible. Only go to 4+ layers when you genuinely need the routing density or signal integrity.

Quantity: More Boards, Lower Per-Unit Cost

The per-unit cost drops dramatically as quantity increases. Here's why: the setup cost (panelization, tooling, first-article inspection) is the same whether you make 5 boards or 500.

If you're prototyping, ordering 10pcs instead of 5 usually costs only $1.50 more but doubles your stock. Always worth it.

Hidden Costs to Watch Out For

  • Shipping — Some factories advertise low board prices but charge high shipping. Always ask for the all-in price (DDP).
  • Stencil — If you're doing SMT assembly, you need a stencil. Some charge $10-20 extra. We include it free.
  • Setup / Tooling Fees — Some brokers add a "setup fee" per order. This shouldn't exist for standard boards.
  • Re-spin costs — If your DFM review finds issues and you need to re-submit, some factories charge a re-spin fee. We don't.
  • Custom solder mask color — Standard green is free. Black, white, yellow, and purple cost $5-10 extra per order.
The cheapest board price isn't always the cheapest order. Always ask for the total cost including shipping, stencil, and any fees. Or just send your Gerber files to us and we'll give you the real number upfront.
