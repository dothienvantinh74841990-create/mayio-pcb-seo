PCB Assembly Costs Explained: Where Your Money Actually Goes

Published June 10, 2026 · By Mayio Engineering Team · 6 min read

Getting a PCB quote back and wondering why it costs what it costs? Here's exactly where your money goes when you order PCB assembly — and where you can save.

The Cost Breakdown

A PCB assembly quote has four main components:

ComponentWhat You Pay ForTypical Cost
PCB FabricationBoard material + copper etching + solder mask + silkscreen$2-50 (depending on specs)
ComponentsResistors, capacitors, ICs, connectors, etc.Varies by BOM
Assembly LaborSMT placement + soldering + inspection$0.01-0.05/part
StencilSteel stencil for solder paste$10-25 (often included)

What Drives Component Costs Up

  • Rare or specialty parts — Custom ASICs, FPGA, sensors
  • Supply chain issues — Shortage = price spike
  • Small quantity pricing — Buying 10 ICs costs more per unit than 1000
  • Multi-source — Same part from Digi-Key vs LCSC can vary 30%

How to Reduce Assembly Costs

  1. Use common components — 0402/0603 resistors, standard caps, SOIC packages
  2. Order more boards — Fixed costs spread across more units
  3. Use a component supplier with good pricing — We source from LCSC, Digi-Key, Mouser
  4. Optimize your BOM — Fewer unique parts = cheaper assembly
  5. Skip unnecessary conformal coating — Only coat what needs protection

Real Example: IoT Sensor Board (50pcs)

  • 4-layer PCB (40×30mm): $0.55/board
  • 45 components (all common 0402/0603/SOIC): $1.80/board
  • SMT assembly: $0.45/board
  • Stencil + testing: $0.30/board
  • Total: ~$3.10/board
  • With DDP shipping: ~$3.50/board

Real Example: Raspberry Pi HAT (10pcs)

  • 4-layer PCB (65×56mm): $1.20/board
  • 62 components (mixed packages): $4.50/board
  • SMT assembly: $0.62/board
  • Stencil + testing: $0.50/board
  • Total: ~$6.82/board

Free Things You Shouldn't Pay For

ItemWhat to Expect
DFM ReviewShould always be free
StencilMost manufacturers include it
Setup FeeShould NOT exist for standard boards
Re-spin FeeCharged by some for DFM corrections
Test ReportBasic AOI report should be included
Bottom line: Assembly cost is driven by component count and board complexity. The best way to save is to use common parts, order in reasonable quantities, and choose a manufacturer that doesn't charge hidden fees.
