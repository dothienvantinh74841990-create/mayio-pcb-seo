Frequently Asked Questions

What is turnkey PCBA?

Turnkey PCBA means the manufacturer handles everything: PCB fabrication, component sourcing, solder paste assembly, and testing. You just send your Gerber files and BOM — they deliver fully assembled boards.

How long does turnkey PCBA take?

Typical lead time is 7-12 working days for standard SMT assembly including component procurement. Express services are available for 5-7 day turnaround on common components.

Can I supply my own components?

Yes. You can supply your own components (known as 'consigned' assembly), or have the manufacturer source everything (full turnkey). Consigned saves on procurement fees but requires you to manage supply chain.

What's the minimum order quantity?

No minimum. We support prototype quantities (1-10 boards) all the way to production runs (10,000+). Pricing scales with volume.

Do you do quality testing on assembled boards?

Yes. Every PCBA order includes automated optical inspection (AOI). We also offer functional testing (ICT), in-circuit testing, and burn-in testing as additional services.

What file formats do you accept?

Gerber (RS-274X) for PCB, BOM in Excel/CSV format, and pick-and-place (CPL) file. KiCad, Eagle, Altium, and OrCAD exports all work.

Send us your Gerber files and BOM — we'll handle the rest.
